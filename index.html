📖 1. INTRODUCTION

- What are recommender systems?

A recommendation system is a specialized type of information filtering system, implemented to predict users preferences towards specific items or content, and deliver personalized suggestions to users based on these predictions (Resnick & Varian, 1997). The core purpose of these systems is to resolve the information overload and seek relevant content efficiently with the large datasets in order to boost user engagement rate and retention for the platforms. It is critical for digital platforms, for instance, Netflix, Amazon, and TaoBao (Sarwat & Mokbel, 2018).

As Wikipedia contributors stated(2025), in order to curate personalized experiences, the recommendation system is heavily relied on by modern digital platforms. There are four widely used recommendation systems. The collaborative filtering focuses on user-item interaction patterns similarities, while content-based filtering recommends items based on similar features that the user has previously preferred. Additionally, hybrid systems combine collaborative and content-based methods, and knowledge-based systems discover user needs and habits with item attributes (Melville & Sindhwani, n.d.).

- Why are they important in business?

By ensuring measurable business value, for instance, countable engagement rate, conversion rate and customer retention, the recommendation system matches users with relevant content and items. It delivers personalized experiences that correlate individual preferences. It results in higher sales, click-through rates (CTR), and overall user satisfaction while reducing information overload (Jannach & Jugovac, 2019). Therefore, the business company can adjust their marketing strategies based on the data.

- Historical evolution

Recommender systems evolved from early methods like collaborative filtering and content-based filtering to hybrid frameworks. Deep learning has become the dominant approach for large-scale industrial deployment since 2025 (Wang & Liu, 2025).
