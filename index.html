📖 1. INTRODUCTION

- What are recommender systems?

A recommendation system is a specialized type of information filtering system, implemented to predict users preferences towards specific items or content, and deliver personalized suggestions to users based on these predictions (Resnick & Varian, 1997). The core purpose of theses system is to resolve the information overload and seek relevant content efficiently with the large datasets in order to boost user engagement rate and retention for the platforms. It is critical for digital platforms, for instance, Netflix, Amazon, and TaoBao (Sarwat & Mokbel, 2018).

As mentioned by Wikipedia contributors (2025), modern digital platforms rely heavily on the recommendation system to curate personalized experiences, four primary types of recommendation systems are widely used. The collaborative filtering, which relies on user-item interaction patterns and similarities between users or items; content-based filtering, which recommends items with similar features that user has previously preferred; hybrid systems that combine collaborative and content-based methods to mitigate limitations of individual approaches; and knowledge-based systems that use explicit domain knowledge to match user needs with item attributes (Melville & Sindhwani, n.d.).

- Why are they important in business?

Recommender systems ensure measurable business value through countable engagement rate, conversion rate and customer retention. It enables platforms to match users with relevant content and items, which delivers personalized experiences that align with individual preferences. It results in higher sales, click-through rates (CTR), and overall user satisfaction while reducing information overload (Jannach & Jugovac, 2019). Therefore, the business company can adjust their marketing strategies based on the data.

- Historical evolution

Recommender systems evolved from early methods like collaborative filtering and content-based filtering to hybrid frameworks. Deep learning has become the dominant approach for large-scale industrial deployment since 2025 (Wang & Liu, 2025).
