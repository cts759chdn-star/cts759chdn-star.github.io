⚙️ 2. What Does "Large-Scale" Mean?

Large-scale recommendation systems ingesting and processing petabytes of user interaction data daily and provide service to millions of users with high-quality, personalized recommendations in real time. Therefore, they require end-to-end scalability across data storage, model training, and inference to ensure the scale of modern web and video platforms (Covington et al., 2016).

The major challenges for large-scale recommender systems is the difficulties in using simple algorithms that work for small datasets due to high concurrent user traffic, massive data volume, and real-time response requirements. Key issues from cold-start problems to data sparsity may occur for new users or items, high computational costs, low scalability, and trade-offs between recommendation accuracy and diversity (Covington et al., 2016; Jannach & Jugovac, 2019). Moreover, the ways to maintain user privacy, transparency and fairness are still be the vital challenges in industrial deployments (Wang & Liu, 2025).
